# Website Audit Prompt — Ana Fedel

## Optimized Prompt

You are a senior digital strategy consultant, UX auditor, SEO specialist, conversion copywriter, and performance optimization expert.

Your task is to perform a complete professional audit of my website:

**https://anafedel.com**

The website belongs to Ana Fedel, a nutritionist. The goal is to make the website more beautiful, premium, trustworthy, easy to use, visible on Google, persuasive, mobile-first, fast-loading, and effective at converting visitors into paying clients.

This audit must be highly valuable, detailed, practical, and based on modern market standards. Do not give generic advice. Analyze the website as if my professional future, brand positioning, and client acquisition depend on it.

Evaluate the website against the standards of:

- Premium wellness clinics
- Boutique nutrition brands
- High-converting personal brand websites
- Modern healthcare and nutrition service websites
- Mobile-first service business websites
- SEO-optimized local professional websites

Your analysis must cover the following areas:

---

## 1. Overall First Impression

Analyze what a new visitor feels within the first 5 seconds of entering the website.

Evaluate:

- Whether the site immediately communicates who I am
- What service I offer
- Who I help
- Why someone should trust me
- Whether the visual impression feels premium, professional, modern, and aligned with a nutritionist brand
- Whether the site creates emotional connection and desire to book a consultation

Give specific recommendations to improve the first impression.

---

## 2. Visual Design and Brand Perception

Evaluate the current design quality based on market standards.

Analyze:

- Color palette
- Typography
- Spacing
- Layout
- Visual hierarchy
- Use of images
- Logo placement
- Buttons
- Section structure
- Overall elegance and professionalism
- Whether the website looks premium or generic
- Whether the design feels aligned with a boutique nutrition/wellness brand

Compare the website with premium wellness clinics, feminine luxury wellness brands, and high-end personal brand websites.

For every recommendation, explain the goal behind it using one or more of these categories:

- Trust
- Beauty
- Clarity
- SEO
- Speed
- Conversion
- Mobile usability

---

## 3. Mobile-First Usability

Assume that most visitors are accessing the website from a cellphone.

Analyze:

- Mobile layout
- Readability on small screens
- Button size and placement
- Menu/navigation usability
- Scrolling experience
- Section order
- WhatsApp/contact accessibility
- Whether the booking path is simple
- Whether the mobile experience feels premium and smooth

Give practical recommendations to improve the mobile version first. Desktop should be considered secondary but still acceptable.

---

## 4. General User Experience and Usability

Analyze how easy it is for a visitor to understand, navigate, and take action.

Evaluate:

- Navigation clarity
- Homepage structure
- Service explanation
- Booking flow
- Contact options
- Calls to action
- Trust signals
- Repeated points of friction
- Confusing sections
- Missing information
- Whether the visitor knows what to do next

Identify usability problems and explain how to fix them.

---

## 5. Conversion Strategy

Analyze how well the website turns visitors into clients.

Evaluate:

- Whether the offer is clear
- Whether the site explains the value of the consultation
- Whether the calls to action are persuasive
- Whether the website reduces objections
- Whether it builds enough trust before asking the visitor to book
- Whether there are enough conversion points throughout the page
- Whether WhatsApp/contact buttons are strategically placed
- Whether the service feels valuable enough to justify the price

Suggest ways to increase bookings, leads, and client inquiries.

Include recommendations for:

- Stronger CTA texts
- Better service presentation
- Better section order
- More persuasive trust-building elements
- Better client journey
- Better positioning of consultation and follow-up services

---

## 6. Copywriting and Messaging

Audit the website copy.

Analyze:

- Clarity
- Emotional appeal
- Persuasiveness
- Professional authority
- Tone of voice
- Whether the copy speaks directly to the ideal client
- Whether the copy explains benefits instead of only features
- Whether it makes the visitor feel understood
- Whether it creates desire to book a consultation

Suggest improved copy for important website sections, including:

- Hero headline
- Hero subheadline
- Main call-to-action button
- About section
- Services section
- Consultation explanation
- Trust/authority section
- Final call-to-action

The tone should be:

- Premium
- Warm
- Professional
- Feminine
- Clear
- Trustworthy
- Human
- Persuasive without sounding aggressive

---

## 7. SEO Audit

Analyze the website for SEO visibility and market standards.

Evaluate:

- Page titles
- Meta descriptions
- Heading structure
- Keyword usage
- Local SEO opportunities
- Service keywords
- Nutrition-related search intent
- Internal linking
- Image alt text
- Content depth
- Blog/content opportunities
- Google visibility potential
- How the website can attract more clients organically

Suggest SEO improvements for a nutritionist website, especially for attracting clients searching for nutrition consultations, weight loss, healthy eating, reeducation, and local nutrition services.

Include recommended keywords in Portuguese, because the target audience is likely Brazilian.

Organize keywords by:

- Main service keywords
- Local SEO keywords
- Problem-aware keywords
- Desire-based keywords
- Blog/article keyword opportunities

---

## 8. Page Loading Speed and Technical Performance

Evaluate potential loading speed issues and their impact on SEO and conversion.

Analyze:

- Image optimization
- File size
- Unnecessary scripts
- Fonts
- Mobile loading experience
- Core Web Vitals considerations
- Layout stability
- Performance risks that may affect Google ranking

Give practical recommendations that a developer can implement.

Explain why each change matters for SEO, user experience, or conversion.

---

## 9. Trust and Authority

Analyze whether the site gives enough reasons for a visitor to trust me.

Evaluate whether the site includes or should include:

- Professional credentials
- CRN information
- Real photos
- Testimonials
- Before/after alternatives that comply with professional ethics
- Patient journey explanation
- Methodology
- Social proof
- Media or professional highlights
- FAQ section
- Privacy and professional responsibility signals

Suggest what trust elements should be added or improved.

---

## 10. Content and Section Structure

Review the current structure of the website and suggest a better section order if needed.

Recommend an ideal homepage structure for higher conversion.

For each section, explain:

- Purpose
- What content it should include
- What emotional or strategic goal it serves
- Whether it improves trust, beauty, clarity, SEO, speed, or conversion

---

## 11. Competitive and Market Standard Comparison

Compare my website against what is expected from:

- Premium nutritionists
- Boutique health clinics
- High-ticket wellness professionals
- Modern personal brands

Identify where the site feels below market standard and what should be improved to look more premium and competitive.

---

## 12. Priority Action Plan

Create a prioritized action plan divided into:

### Immediate fixes

Changes that should be done first and can quickly improve clarity, trust, mobile usability, or conversion.

### High-impact improvements

Changes that require more work but can significantly improve SEO, branding, and client acquisition.

### Premium upgrades

Changes that would make the website feel more sophisticated, boutique, and high-end.

---

## 13. Final Checklist for Execution

Create a final practical checklist that I can give to:

- A designer
- A developer
- A copywriter
- An SEO specialist

The checklist must be clear, specific, and actionable.

---

## Output Format

Deliver the audit as a professional consulting report with clear headings.

Use this structure:

1. Executive Summary
2. First Impression Audit
3. Design and Brand Audit
4. Mobile Usability Audit
5. User Experience Audit
6. Conversion Audit
7. Copywriting Audit
8. SEO Audit
9. Speed and Technical Performance Audit
10. Trust and Authority Audit
11. Recommended Homepage Structure
12. Competitive Market Comparison
13. Prioritized Action Plan
14. Final Implementation Checklist

For each recommendation, include:

- The problem
- Why it matters
- The recommended change
- The expected impact
- The goal category: Trust, Beauty, Clarity, SEO, Speed, Conversion, or Mobile usability

Be direct, strategic, and practical. Do not be superficial. Do not only compliment the website. Be honest, but constructive. Focus on what will make the website more premium, more visible, more persuasive, and more effective at attracting clients.

---

## Optional Enhancer

After the audit, create a redesigned homepage wireframe in text format, with suggested section titles, copy examples, CTA buttons, and visual direction for each section.
